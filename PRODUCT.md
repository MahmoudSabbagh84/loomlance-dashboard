# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Freelance software developers and small dev studios (solo to ~5 people) running the business side of their work. They're technically fluent, time-poor, and admin-averse — they'd rather be building. Their context: they drop into LoomLance between or alongside client work to manage clients, scope contracts, track time and expenses, send invoices, and get paid, then get back to their IDE.

The job to be done: **run a professional freelance business with the least possible friction and the fewest possible tools** — "code, ship, get paid, repeat." Plans: Solo (free) / Freelancer (tier_1) / Studio (tier_2).

## Product Purpose

LoomLance is the post-login app where the work happens: clients + CRM, projects (kanban), contracts (+PDF), invoicing (PDF + email + online pay), time tracking, expenses, recurring invoices, and reports — one source of truth that replaces a dozen disconnected tools. It's the **developer-first** alternative to generic freelance/accounting software.

Success: a freelancer can go from "new client" to "paid invoice" inside one calm, fast app, and trust it with their money and their client relationships.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, trustworthy, precise.** This software handles people's money and client relationships, so it projects quiet confidence — never flashy, never anxious, never shouting. Premium but understated: high-craft and deliberate, it gets out of the way. The voice is plain, exact, and respectful of the user's time — a competent peer, not a cheerful mascot.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template** — gradient hero, identical icon-card grids, Bootstrap-default spacing, "could be any startup." If a screen looks template- or AI-generated, it's wrong.
- **Cluttered enterprise accounting** (QuickBooks / SAP / legacy tools) — dropdown soup, dense intimidating forms, joyless gray density. LoomLance must feel lighter, calmer, and more focused than what it replaces.
- Also explicitly avoid: playful/consumer cartoonishness and crypto/neon-dark hype — both undercut trust for a money tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through restraint.** Calm surfaces, generous space, one confident accent. Nothing competes with the user's data. Money software earns trust by being quiet and predictable, not by decoration.
2. **The data is the hero.** Invoices, totals, clients, hours. Chrome recedes; content and numbers (tabular, aligned, legible) lead. Decoration that doesn't serve comprehension is removed.
3. **Fewer tools, fewer steps.** Every flow shortens the path from work done to money received — sensible defaults, autosave, no dead ends. The product reduces admin; it never adds it.
4. **Production-grade, not prototype.** Real loading / empty / error states, correct money math, accessible, responsive. Craft is how the product signals reliability with the user's livelihood.
5. **Developer-first clarity.** Speak the user's language and respect their fluency — precise labels, keyboard-friendly, no hand-holding fluff.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast (≥ 3:1 for large/bold); always-visible focus states; full keyboard navigation; honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with crossfade/instant alternatives; never rely on color alone for status (pair with icon or text). Both light and dark themes must hold AA contrast (design tokens are defined for both).
